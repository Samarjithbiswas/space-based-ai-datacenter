# Cover letter

Dear Editors,

Please consider the enclosed manuscript, *Space-Based Data Center Infrastructure: A Multi-Physics
Feasibility and Survivability Study*, for publication.

Space-based artificial-intelligence data centers have moved from concept to early flight hardware
within the past year, yet the public case rests largely on a non-peer-reviewed vendor preprint and on
optimistic single-point projections. This manuscript provides what has been missing: an integrated,
first-order, and fully reproducible systems-engineering analysis that joins the thermal, orbital,
radiation, debris, communications, power, propulsion, reliability, compute, and economic subsystems
into one consistent design point and propagates the dominant uncertainties.

The central findings reframe the problem. Radiative cooling, widely assumed to be the hard problem,
is the mature part of the architecture; the binding constraints are instead orbital-debris
survivability in a tight formation, mandatory active disposal, and launch economics. The manuscript
identifies a structural formation paradox, in which the spacing required by the optical links is the
same spacing that makes a collision cascade dangerous, and it replaces the usual single cost-parity
date with a Monte-Carlo credibility distribution showing that parity at the assumed price is unlikely
under most assumptions rather than merely late.

The work is original and the author's own. Every figure and number is regenerated by an open-source
model with an automated test suite, released under a permissive licence; the model and the full
derivations are available for review. The manuscript states its first-order limitations explicitly and
maintains a strict distinction between demonstrated capability and projection. Every quantitative claim
derives from the open code or cited primary sources, all equations were independently verified, and the
author is solely responsible for the content.

The manuscript has not been published elsewhere and is not under consideration by another venue.

Thank you for your consideration.

Sincerely,

Samarjith Biswas, PhD
