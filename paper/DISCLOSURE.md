# Methods, reproducibility, and AI-use disclosure

This statement accompanies *Space-Based Data Center Infrastructure: A Multi-Physics Approach* and its
condensed manuscript. It is written to satisfy the transparency requirements of AIAA, IEEE, and
Elsevier venues.

## Methods

The study is a first-order systems-engineering analysis. Each subsystem is modeled with closed-form
or low-order numerical relations (orbital mechanics, atmospheric drag and decay, gray-body thermal
balance and a series conduction chain, trapped-particle dose and single-event-upset rates, a Poisson
collision model, an optical link budget, solar-array and attitude sizing, the rocket equation and a
Hohmann disposal maneuver, exponential and k-of-n reliability, and discounted-cash-flow economics).
These are sizing and risk-ranking models, not high-fidelity flight-design tools; the analysis does not
include a multi-node thermal finite-element model, agency debris-tool runs (NASA ORDEM, ESA MASTER and
DRAMA), a closed-loop attitude-control simulation, micrometeoroid Whipple sizing, or a full
time-domain mission simulation. These limitations are stated in the manuscript.

Inputs are sourced, with confidence levels, from public agency models and datasets (NRLMSIS, the
AE/AP and SHIELDOSE radiation models, ORDEM and MASTER debris fluxes, the FCC disposal rule), vendor
documentation, standard textbooks, and the reference-architecture preprint (arXiv:2511.19468). The
reference preprint is itself non-peer-reviewed and vendor-authored; conclusions that depend on it are
flagged as such. A strict distinction is maintained between demonstrated capability and projection,
and forward-looking quantities are reported as distributions with uncertainty bands.

## Reproducibility

Every figure and every load-bearing number is regenerated by an open-source model
(`github.com/Samarjithbiswas/space-based-ai-datacenter`, release v1.0). The model carries 122 automated
tests and a worked-example validation suite (23 checks for the systems model, 14 for the survivability
model). A single command reproduces the full analysis.

## Use of generative AI

Generative AI (Anthropic Claude) was used as a writing and drafting aid: to help draft and expand
prose and derivations, to generate figures and schematic diagrams from the model outputs, and to
assist with editing. It was not used to invent data, results, or sources.

All quantitative claims are produced by the open code or taken from cited primary sources, not from
the language model. Equations were checked independently: a systematic per-chapter verification pass
identified and corrected several algebraic, sign, and numerical errors before release, and the
governing relations are anchored to the textbook sources listed in the references. The author reviewed
and is solely responsible for all content, claims, and conclusions. Generative AI is not, and cannot
be, an author of this work.
